Our Mission

Changing what's possible for science apprentices

ARC exists to change how the world sees science apprenticeships - and to change what's possible for the people who do them.

We support science apprentices and alumni to progress into postgraduate research, higher education, and senior roles in industry, while showing the next generation that an apprenticeship is "not the backup option". It's a launchpad.

Too many talented people are told that apprenticeships are the "safe option." We think they're the smart one.

Mentoring & Guidance

Connecting current apprentices with people who've walked the path - from the lab bench to PhDs, professorships, and industry leadership.

Changing Perceptions

Showing school leavers, parents, and teachers that science apprenticeships are a powerful route into extraordinary careers - not a compromise.

Building Community

Creating a network of science apprentice alumni who support each other, share opportunities, and prove that where you start doesn't define where you end up.

Where It Started

From apprentice to PhD researcher

ARC was founded by Lucy, a science apprentice who was told the same thing many apprentices hear: "It's a good backup plan." She chose to see it differently.

Starting as a laboratory scientist apprentice, Lucy gained her BSc and went on to secure an NIHR-funded PhD at UCL's Great Ormond Street Institute of Child Health, researching tissue engineering and stem cell biology to help children with Hirschsprung disease.

Along the way, she won the Royal Society of Biology's Apprentice of the Year award and was shortlisted for the Wellcome Photography Prize for her scientific imaging. None of it happened despite the apprenticeship. It happened because of it.

"Nobody sat me down and said: you can go from an apprenticeship to a PhD. I had to figure it out myself. ARC exists so the next person doesn't have to."

The path from apprentice to researcher barely existed when Lucy started. There was no roadmap, no community, no one to ask. ARC is here to change that - so that every science apprentice who wants to go further has the support, the role models, and the belief that they can.
