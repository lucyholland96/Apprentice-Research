What Are the Different Apprenticeship Levels?

Understanding Levels 2–7 and what each one gives you.

The Levels at a Glance

There are six apprenticeship levels in England, numbered 2 to 7. Each one maps roughly onto a traditional qualification, but don't let that fool you into thinking they're "lesser" versions — they combine academic learning with real professional experience, which is something a traditional degree alone doesn't give you.

Level 2 — Intermediate Equivalent to GCSEs (grades 4–9). Often a starting point for people new to an industry. No previous experience needed.

Level 3 — Advanced Equivalent to A-levels. This is where most science apprenticeships begin — laboratory technician, food technologist, pharmacy services assistant. You might need GCSEs to get in, but not always.

Level 4 — Higher Equivalent to the first year of an undergraduate degree, or a Higher National Certificate (HNC). You're getting into specialist territory here.

Level 5 — Higher Equivalent to a foundation degree or Higher National Diploma (HND). More specialised, more challenging, and a serious credential in its own right.

Level 6 — Degree Equivalent to a full bachelor's degree. Yes, really. A Level 6 apprenticeship gives you a BSc or BA while you work and earn — with no student debt.

Level 7 — Master's Degree Equivalent to a postgraduate master's. These exist in areas like advanced clinical practice, senior leadership, and some areas of engineering.

So What Does "Equivalent" Actually Mean?

Exactly what it says. A Level 6 degree apprenticeship awards you the same bachelor's degree as someone who went to university full-time. It's the same qualification — you just got there via a different route, with more hands-on experience and without the debt.

The Regulated Qualifications Framework (RQF) sets these levels nationally. They're not "sort of like" traditional qualifications — they sit at the same level, formally and legally.

Which Science Apprenticeships Are Out There?

There are science-relevant apprenticeships at almost every level. Some of the most common include:

  • Level 3: Laboratory Technician, Pharmacy Services Assistant, Food Technologist
  • Level 4: Healthcare Science Associate, Research and Development Technician
  • Level 5: Healthcare Science Practitioner (various specialisms)
  • Level 6: Laboratory Scientist, Healthcare Science Practitioner (Degree), Professional Scientist
  • Level 7: Advanced Clinical Practitioner, and others in development

You can search for all current apprenticeship standards on the Institute for Apprenticeships website or browse available vacancies on Find an Apprenticeship.

Can I Progress Between Levels?

Absolutely. You might start at Level 3, discover you love your field, and progress to a Level 5 or 6, sometimes with the same employer. Some programmes are even designed with built-in progression routes. If your employer has access to the Apprenticeship Levy, they may be able to fund your next level directly.
